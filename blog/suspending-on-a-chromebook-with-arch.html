Suspending on a chromebook with Arch

[link—standalone]

When i installed Arch on my Acer c720 Chromebook for the first time, i noticed that it would not suspend correctly. It suspended fine, but when it resumed, it usually rebooted for some reason.
Since there was not a working solution for me in the Arch Wiki, i am making this article so other new Arch users do not also have to hunt through the Arch Forums for a few hours as i had to.
Note that this article is for new users of Arch who already looked at the Arch Wiki and the solutions did not work. This article also assumes you use GRUB as your bootloader.
Open /etc/default/grub and add 
tpm_tis.interrupts=0 modprobe.blacklist=ehci_hcd,ehci_pci
to the GRUB_CMDLINE_LINUX_DEFAULT line (inside the quote, before the watchdog part if it is there).
Then, rebuild your grub config with grub-mkconfig -o /boot/grub/grub.cfg
Basically what this does is disable a few kernel modules that you dont need and do things that stop it from successfully waking from a suspend. If you do this, you should probably research what these modules do and if you are going to use them, this article is just to point new users in the right direction to fix their suspending.
